    USA government channels financial support directly to West Pokot landslide victims

    - The US government lauded the work of the local authorities and all humanitarian officials supporting the victims - It stated it was directly helping victims of the landslide alongside Kenya Red Cross Society

    13 Apr 2024, 00:00

    - The US government lauded the work of the local authorities and all humanitarian officials supporting the victims

    - It stated it was directly helping victims of the landslide alongside Kenya Red Cross Society

    - The landslide occurred on Saturday, November 23, with the death toll surpassing 50

    - Interior CS Matiang'i confirmed the government also provided relief supplies to the affected families

    The United States government has provided financial help directly to the West Pokot survivors of the deadly mudslide that left more than 50 dead on Saturday, November 23.

    The US lauded the work of local authorities, humanitarian officials and all the agencies who joined to support the victims in the tragedy.

    In a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke, the embassy said it would be supporting the affected families through its partnership with Kenya Red Cross to ensure the funds reach the victims directly.

    "Our profound sympathies go to the affected families of the West Pokot landslide disaster and the US government through its partnership with the Kenya Red Cross is providing financial support directly to the affected people," the statement read.

    The landslide occurred on the pre-dawn of Saturday, November 23, where the avalanche swept more than 50 people into their deaths in three villages in West Pokot.

    Most people were sleeping when the catastrophe happened where many people also their homes and properties.

    Deputy President William Ruto in the company of his lieutenants visited Kapenguria County Teaching and Referral Hospital to comfort the affected.

    However, Interior CS Fred Matiangi' and his Devolution counterpart Eugene Wamalwa stopped their trip to West Pokot midway citing bad weather.

    Matiangi' assured the government is supporting the families with relief supplies despite weather challenges which have rendered some of the roads in the area impassable.
