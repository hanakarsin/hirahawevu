    Uhuru Kenyatta reveals he has family ties in Jamaica

    - President Uhuru arrived in the Caribbean nation on Monday, August 5, for a two-day state visit - The head of state held bilateral talks with his host Andrew Holness, Jamaican prime minister - The two leaders signed an MoU in area of sports, tourism and culture as well as transport

    28 Feb 2024, 00:00

    - President Uhuru arrived in the Caribbean nation on Monday, August 5, for a two-day state visit

    - The head of state held bilateral talks with his host Andrew Holness, Jamaican prime minister

    - The two leaders signed an MoU in area of sports, tourism and culture as well as transport

    - Uhuru invited Jamaican investors and urged them to start seeing Kenya as an economic hub

    President Uhuru Kenyatta and his host Jamaican prime minister Andrew Holness held bilateral talks on Monday, August 5, purposely to deepen ties and economic partnership.

    During a press briefing attended by delegations from the two governments, Uhuru revealed his brother-in-law's mother is Jamaican and she has lived in Kenya for the longest time.

    "We affirm the Jamaican diaspora is everywhere and the prime minister was wondering whether we have some in Kenya. In deed I can confirm given the fact my wife's brother's mother comes from Jamaica and she lives in Kenya," he disclosed.

    Earlier in the day, the leaders signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to work closely in areas of sports with keen interest in athletics, education, transport as well as tourism.

    It was disclosed the two nations were planning to introduce direct flights from Nairobi to Kingston as part of the journey towards a vibrant economic partnership.
