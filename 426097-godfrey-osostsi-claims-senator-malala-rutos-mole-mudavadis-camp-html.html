    • He claims the presumed Mudavadi's close allies are keen on using his popularity after which they will dump him

    Amani National Congress (ANC) party leader Musalia Mudavadi has been cautioned of politicians seeking to ride on his popularity then abandon him in the long run.

    According to nominated member of parliament Godfrey Osotsi, Mudavadi is currently surrounded by conniving politicians who, in the low key, advance the agenda of Deputy President William Ruto.

    The lawmaker pointed out Kakamega senator Cleophas Malala who has been on record insisting Mudavadi must be on the presidential ballot in the upcoming General Election despite the latter seemingly not having the upper hand in the polls.

    "Musalia Mudavadi, you are a leader we respect. You are a leader who can become Kenya's president. But take time to vet those around you. They claim to help you but they are not. These people are moles working for other people. They seat with you inn boardrooms but they are working for other people," said Osotsi.

    The lawmaker added that the said politicians were running an anti-Raila campaign in the western region under the guise of drumming support for Mudavadi, but on the flipside campaigning for Deputy President William Ruto, who will be making his first stab at the presidency.

    According to Osotsi, the politicians led by Malala and Lurambi MP Titus Khamala know that Mudavadi would not have a chance in the presidential race but still vow to charge him up for their own political advantage.

    "They pretend to defend you, they attack Raila Odinga, but in reality, they are not with you. They are with you during the day but when the night comes they shift allegiance to Ruto. They know you can't win but for their own expediency, they have to use you," Osotsi told Mudavadi.

    Mudavadi must be on the presidential ballot

    The Kakamega senator has constantly insisted that the ANC party leader must vie for the country's top seat, dismissing the calls to have him bow for another candidate.

    According to him, Mudavadi is already ripe to take over from President Uhuru Kenyatta, whose term will be lapsing next year.

    "There has been widespread speculation that our party leader will be supporting somebody else in 2022 General Election. We want to affirm here that as Amani National Congress, we gave him the mandate to go and represent us as a presidential candidate; not as a campaign manager, not as a deputy president, not as a prime minister but as a presidential candidate.     Malala is eyeing the Kakamega governorship, and to woo the electorate, his opponents claim he is capitalising on Mudavadi's popularity in the western region.

    The first-term senator has turned into a Raila critique, always calling out the ODM leader for banking on the western electorate whenever he partakes in the presidential contest.
