    Embakasi DCI Boss Simon Mwongela Arrested for Shooting, Killing Watchman

    Embakasi Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss Simon Mwongela has been arrested after he allegedly shot and killed a watchman in Kayole, Nairobi county.

    Mwongela was arrested on Wednesday, May 13, according to a statement issued by Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai.

    The senior cop said investigations into the incident had been launched. He said the suspect would be charged with murder.

    “He (Mwongela) has been arrested and is now under investigation for murder,” Mutyambai said.

    A police officer at the scene said the sad incident happened at 4:30am on Wednesday after the defendant forcefully tried to enter a bar the watchman was guarding.

    “He had gone to the club in the morning and demanded to get in, and when he was told it had been closed, he shot the guard on his way out as he demanded to be shown where his car was.”

    Cases of police brutality and killings have been on the increase in recent years.

    Two officers found guilty of manslaughter

    On Tuesday, May 11, two police officers who shot dead a woman at city park in May 2018 were found guilty of manslaughter.

    Police constables William Chirchir and Godfrey Kirui shot dead the woman who was with her nephew Bernard Chege, who sustained severe injuries on the morning of May 20, 2018.

    While making the ruling on Tuesday, May 11, High Court Judge Stella Mutuku said the two officers used extreme force despite the victim and her nephew posing no threat.

    Mutuku said the duo had not also committed a crime, and the officers thus behaved hastily and shot with the intent to kill. She also pointed out that the law enforcers did not fire in the air or at the tires to deflate them but instead shot at Waiyaki at her passenger seat.

    "All of the officers were armed, and they should have behaved cautiously. I believe there were other ways to intercept the car, and they also broke the law requiring them to protect life," she said in the ruling.
