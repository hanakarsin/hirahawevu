    Uhuru Kenyatta Speaks on BBI Fate: &quot;People Decided to Deny Kenyans What's Theirs&quot;

    President Uhuru Kenyatta has finally broken his silence on the Court of Appeal verdict that upheld the declaration of the High Court that the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) process was unconstitutional.

    • He denied the bill was aimed at prolonging his term or making ODM leader Raila Odinga president
    • According to him, BBI would have improved the livelihoods of Kenyans and made Kenya a more cohesive society

    President Uhuru Kenyatta has finally broken his silence on the Court of Appeal verdict that upheld the declaration of the High Court that the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) process was unconstitutional.

    The president faulted the courts for denying Kenyans what he said was legitimately in their interest.

    He said the bill's opponents chose to politicise it and mistook it as a tool for political contest.

    "The courts ruled the way they ruled and I believe that they were highly misguided in that process. We had politicians who deviated from why we wanted the BBI to it being an issue of competition," stated Uhuru.

    "I am sad that for political reasons, we could actually deny our people something that would have improved their livelihoods and made Kenya a more cohesive society," said the president.

    Uhuru denied the claims that the document had been fronted to help him prolong his term or propel ODM leader Raila Odinga to presidency.

    "I heard some saying the BBI was meant to propel Raila Odinga to be president. He declared his presidency with or without BBI and he's still on the ticket. BBI has nothing to do with his candidacy. It has nothing to do with Uhuru continuing his term because their is no clause in it saying the incumbent president continues his term. It is all propaganda," he said.

    The president said he firmly believed in uniting the country and part of that was by having more leadership positions as proposed in the now-moribund BBI document.

    He also clarified that his meetings with National Super Alliance principals had nothing to do with succession politics, further stating why Ruto was excluded from the BBI process.

    "He ( Ruto) has been opposed to the (BBI) process. Meetings are not about selecting a candidate. You cannot bring someone who is opposed and does not want to see things happen on board. You cannot force something down someoneâ€™s throat," he said.

    Court of Appeal anti-BBI decision

    A seven-judge Court of Appeal bench on Friday, August 20, found the process unconstitutional and handed the promoters a blow that could potentially stop the sounds and drumbeats of BBI reggae.

    The voluminous judgment not only presented far-reaching political and legal implications but also exposed President Uhuru Kenyatta and his role in the amendment of the 2010 Constitution.

    The Court of Appeal president Daniel Musinga and judge Patrick Kiage, in their lengthy submissions, agreed with the bench that the president's role is limited to protecting and defending the Constitution.

    "Under our constitutional architecture, the president cannot initiate the process of amendment of the constitution as a popular initiative. His intentions were noble but the execution was not in line with the constitution," Musinga ruled.

    On his part, Justice Patrick Kiage argued that although the president enjoys certain powers as provided for in the constitution, he does not shoulder any obligation to initiate constitutional changes.

    "His duty is to obey and defend it in keeping with the oath of allegiance.....The spirit of the constitution must preside and permeate the process of judicial interpretation and judicial discretion," he stated.

    Welcoming the decision, Raila, a chief proponent of the bill, said it was now time for the country to move on.

    However, he said the quest of a united nation borne in BBI would continue regardless of the verdict.
