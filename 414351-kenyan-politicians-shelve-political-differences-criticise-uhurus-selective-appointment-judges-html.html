    Politicians from across the political divide have joined hands to criticise President Uhuru Kenyatta over his selective appointment of judges on Thursday, June 3.

    In a gazette notice, the head of state appointed 34 new judges and rejected six nominees despite a recommendation by the Judicial Service Commission.

    Some of the biggest casualties were High Court judges George Odunga and Joel Ngugi who in May were among the five-judge bench that dealt a blow to the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) by declaring it unconstitutional, null and void.

    Other judges who failed to make it to the Court of Appeal were Weldon Korir and Aggrey Muchelule.

    Magistrates Cheruiyot Judith Omange and Evans Makori on the other hand were dropped from the High Court list.

    Here were some of the reactions from leaders:

    Rarieda lawmaker Otiende Amollo of ODM said:

    "On this, we must be plain, the president cannot select which judges to gazette after JSC recommendation."

    Elgeyo Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen tweeted:

    "I am not so worried about what Uhuru has done on judicial appointments. His time is running out. I am more worried by the thought that all judicial appointments in the future will depend on the wishes of the president. makes nonsense of judicial independence under our constitution."

    Homa Bay Town's Peter Kaluma noted:

    Read also

    "I don't agree with the BBI judgement, but I hope it is not the reason judge Odunga and Judge Ngugi have not been appointed to the Court of Appeal. Together with Judge Muchelule and Korir, they are among our best brains on the bench. Waiting to see how CJ Koome will resolve this!"

    Narc Kenya party leader Martha Karua said:

    "Cherry-picking of @jsckenya appointees may amount to deliberate intimidation of @Kenyajudiciary especially in view of the uncalled for attacks of the institution by the president in Kisumu coupled with the pending #BBIAppeal we must stand firm to #LindaKatiba."

    Nominated senator Millicent Omanga opined:

    "The president cannot cherry-pick who he should or shouldn't appoint as a judge. JSC must issue a statement to rebuke this illegal action by the president. He is settling scores. Bad for our country."

    Former Mukurweini MP Kabando was Kabando added:

    "President Uhuru has predictably trashed the constitution. CJ Martha koome should protect CoK by refusing to swear any until all 40 are gazetted. The 34 judges should decline being sworn until all 40 are gazetted. The real test on Chief Justice martha koome starts today. Trailblazer or puppet?
