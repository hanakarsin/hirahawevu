    Bandits kill police officer who survived accident that killed his 7 colleagues

    A police officer who escaped death by a whisker in April 2020 has been killed by bandits in Baringo North.

    The officer was shot dead on Thursday, December 31, when the armed bandits raided the police camp while he was collecting firewood with 10 other law enforcers.

    A report by Citizen TV indicated the officer was shot on the back while fleeing from the gangsters who ambushed them on New Year's eve.

    Locals who are still recovering from the shock of the incident were apprehensive another attack may erupt.

    The deceased police officer survived an accident on April 30, 2020, after heavy floods swept away the lorry he and his other colleagues were in.

    The lorry ferrying the officers drawn from the anti-stock theft unit was overwhelmed by flash floods at Chemoe area resulting in the tragic incident.

    Rift Valley Regional Commissioner George Natembeya said the lorry had 12 police officers when the accident happened.
