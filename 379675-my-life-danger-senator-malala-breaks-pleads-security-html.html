    My life is in danger: Senator Malala breaks down as he pleads for more security

    - Kakamega senator Cleophas Malala claimed that about five police offices have been trailing him - Malala stated he has been living in fear that his life may be snatched from him any time as he walks along the streets

    30 May 2024, 00:00

    - Kakamega senator Cleophas Malala claimed that about five police offices have been trailing him

    - Malala stated he has been living in fear that his life may be snatched from him any time as he walks along the streets

    - The Senate directed Police Inspector General Hillary Mutyambai to assign Malala security detail of his choice

    Kakamega senator Cleophas Malala has once again informed Senate Speaker Ken Lusaka that his life is in danger.

    Malala on Monday, September 14, broke into tears as he addressed the House claiming people belonging to law enforcement agencies were trailing him.

    Life in danger

    The Senate Committee on Security asked Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss George Kinoti to appear before it.

    It also directed Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai to provide the lawmaker with additional security officers of his choice.

    "It is sad for me to walk around the streets of Kakamega and Kenya knowing that I am going to die the following day Mr Chair. The most painful thing is that I hear respectable leaders claiming that I stage-managed my previous arrest. Mr. chair I carry a heavy heart with me," Malala told the Senate Committee on Security chaired by Garissa senator Yusuf Haji as he cried and walked away after becoming too emotional.

    Malala's claims that police were after his life came about three weeks after his dramatic night arrest from his Kitengela home.

    Division of revenue debate

    Malala linked his arrest to his sustained opposition to the division of revenue formula proposed by the Commission on Revenue Allocation (CRA) and backed by President Uhuru Kenyatta.

    The formula, which will guide how the 47 counties will share the KSh 316.5 billion earmarked for devolved units for 2020-2021 financial year, premises population as the major determining factor for fiscal allocations.

    If implemented, about 18 counties would lose millions of shillings compared to what they got during the 2019-2021 financial year with the hardest-hit being devolved units from the coast and northeastern regions.

    The debate is still on the floor of the Senate, far from reaching a consensus, having flopped for a record 11 times.

    Senator Sakaja

    Nairobi senator Johnson Sakaja, who is among senators who have staged a spirited campaign against the formula, had also earlier claimed his life was in danger.

    Sakaja said a police car was trailing him an at one time, forced him to leave his house at 4am and spent the whole morning at Parliament's basement parking fearing for his life.

    While appearing before Senate Committee on Security, Interior CS Fred Matiang'i dismissed claims the arrests were linked Senate proceedings.

    Matiang'i said the lawmakers had been summoned in their respective counties to record statements over insecurity and flouting of COVID-19 rules but they failed to honour the summons.
