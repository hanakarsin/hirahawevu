    Kenyans Shower Mama Ida Odinga With Praises as She Celebrates Birthday: &quot;Golden Heart&quot;

    The Kenyan internet was excited on Tuesday, August 24, as Mama Ida Odinga, the wife to former prime minister Raila Odinga, was marking her 71st birthday.

    The netizens took to Twitter to celebrate Ida, who is lauded for being a great support to Raila's outstanding political career.

    A quick glimpse at the microblogging site confirms Ida was among the trending topics, Kenyans wishing her every luck as she continues to age, and gracefully so.

    Raila's lovely message to Ida

    On the day, the ODM chief led Kenyans in celebrating Ida, who made yet another milestone.

    Captioning her heydays' photo, Raila, who is currently out of the country, admired his better half, wishing her luck.

    "Cha cha cha NyarOlwande; Happy birthday my dear," exclaimed Raila.

    Also, to celebrate Ida were her children, wishing for God's presence in her life.

    @Raila Odinga Junior said:

    "Whatever makes you feel happy and loved Mama, that’s what I wish for you today. Happy birthday Mama” May the Lord always be with you, protect you and strengthen you with his presence."

    @Winnie Odinga:

    "Mama! I wish you all the happiness in the world!"

    Ida is an alumnus of the University of Nairobi, from where she graduated with a Bachelor of Education Arts degree.

    She is a teacher by profession and began her career at the Kenya High School, a renowned national girls' school.

    She taught at the institution for more than two decades, producing some of Kenya's powerful leaders, among them the late Joyce Laboso, who by the time of her death was the governor of Bomet county.

    She later came to found the league of Kenyan women voters in 1991.

    The league was charged with promoting opportunities for women in the then male-dominated political field.

    In 2003, she joined East African Spectre Ltd as the managing director.

    The company is a Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) Cylinder manufacturer based in Industrial Area, Nairobi.
