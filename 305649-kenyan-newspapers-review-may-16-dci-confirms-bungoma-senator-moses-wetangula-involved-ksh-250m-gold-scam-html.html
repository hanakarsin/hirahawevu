    Kenyan newspapers review for May 16: DCI confirms Bungoma Senator Moses Wetang'ula was involved in K

    The fake gold sting being carried out by DCI sleuths continued to take prominence in national newspapers.     25 Jun 2024, 00:00

    The fake gold sting being carried out by DCI sleuths continued to take prominence in national newspapers.

    The Thursday, May 16 dailies also reported widely on the clash between the Ministry of Education and the Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT) over implementation of the new Competence Based Curriculum (CBC).

    1. People Daily

    Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss George Kinoti has confirmed that Bungoma Senator Moses Wetang'ula was involved in the gold deal that saw rulers from the United Arab Emirate (UAE) lose over KSh 250 million.

    Kinoti said investigations were underway to reveal whether Wetang'ula was a victim or an accomplice in the racket.

    Wetang'ula had earlier claimed he was just a victim adding that he had also lost a lot of money to fraudulent gold dealers.

    The UAE, according to the daily, wrote to President Uhuru Kenyatta asking him to take action against Kenyans who had duped the royal family in the scam.

    2. Nation

    Kisumu Governor Anyang' Nyong'o and Muhoroni MP Onyango K'Oyoo have apologised to ODM leader Raila Odinga following an incident where residents heckled him.

    Raila's speech was disrupted by Ombei residents on Monday, May 13, when he informed them that a section of land in the area would be set aside for construction of an industrial park.

    Nation reported that Nyong'o and K'Oyoo termed the incident as barbaric.

    Rarieda MP Otiende Amollo had ealier dismissed reports that Raila was heckled at his political backyard.

    Amollo said the development was misreported.

    3. Taifa Leo

    Taifa Leo reported that influence of DP William Ruto has been reducing sharply since the March 2018 handshake between President Uhuru and Raila.

    According to the daily, before the truce, any leader or investor who wanted to access Uhuru had to go through Ruto.

    However, after the handshake, everything has changed and anyone who wants to speak to the head of state has to knock the door of the former premier.

    Dozens of leaders from the Jubilee predominant regions of Mount Kenya and Rift Valley have been flocking Raila's Capitol Hill offices with their papers.

    They have been asking the ODM chief to forward them to the head of state for job considerations.

    4. Standard

    Former Deputy Chief Justice Nancy Baraza has accused the Judiciary of exercising doubled standards.

    Baraza who resigned following an incident where she had an altercation with a security guard at a mall, said her colleagues used the incident to send her home.

    According to her, some of the practicing jurists have been involved in more ugly scenes and if the same yardstick used in her case was applied on them, then they would be rotting in jail.

    Baraza who now serves as a don at UoN insisted that the incident that led to her resignation was a small one but it was magnified by judges who catalysed the stories in the media..

    5. Star

    Besieged Kiambu Governor Ferdinand Waititu has told his critiques that they would go insane or take their own lives if they know the source and expanse of his wealth.

    Waititu was responding to claims that he has acquired buildings valued at over KSh 2 billion in Nairobi shortly after becoming governor.

    According to the county boss, all his wealth is acquired in transparent ways.

    He termed critiques as jealous and challenged them to file a case in court if they have evidence to substantiate their claims.
