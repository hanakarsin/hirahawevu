    KQ to Lease 2 Jets to Congo Airways In Bid to Bolster Aviation Ties with DRC

    National carrier Kenya Airways (KQ) has entered into an aircraft lease agreement with Congo Airways and leased it two of its jets, TUKO.co.ke has learned. Under the deal, KQ will lease two Embraer E190 jets to Congo Airways, boosting domestic operations and flight frequencies in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

    20 Aug 2024, 00:00
    • The move fulfils an MoU signed between Kenya and DRC in April 2021
    • Kenya Airways CEO Allan Kilavuka said the deal will create a model for cooperation between the other African airlines
    • KQ has also commenced direct cargo flights from Johannesburg, South Africa to Lubumbashi, DRC

    National carrier Kenya Airways (KQ) has entered into an aircraft lease agreement with Congo Airways and leased it two of its jets, TUKO.co.ke has learned.

    Under the deal, KQ will lease two Embraer E190 jets to Congo Airways, boosting domestic operations and flight frequencies in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

    This is part of a cargo codeshare partnership that Kenya Airways has initiated with the national carrier of the DRC.

    In a statement, KQ said the partnership will strengthen collaboration and bolsters aviation ties between Kenya and the DRC.

    It is also fulfills the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed between Kenya Airways and Congo Airways in April 2021 in Kinshasa and witnessed by President Uhuru Kenyatta and DRC's President Felix Tshisekedi.

    Commenting on the deal, KQ Group Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer Allan Kilavuka said the partnership will create a model for cooperation between two African Airlines, which will contribute to Africa's socio-economic development.

    "This is an important step in enhancing cooperation to increase air connectivity and offer greater passenger and cargo options between the two countries. The timing of this agreement is correct, considering the severe impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the aviation industry, as it will increase the utilisation of our aircraft,” Kilavuka said.

    Congo Airways’ CEO Pascal Kasongo Mwema Mwenda said increased air connectivity will offer more choices to passengers in the DRC and between the two countries.

    “It will also provide better connectivity to international destinations, enabling both countries to fly to a better future and build a resilient aviation industry that can stand the test of time," he added.

    The leasing agreement comes even as KQ has announced the commencement of direct cargo flights from Johannesburg, South Africa to Lubumbashi, DRC.

    Kilavuka said the new route is expected to provide further connections to additional points within the DRC and Africa.

    “Cargo has provided new opportunities in the face of the pandemic, and we are keen to continue building our operations and leveraging partnerships,” he stated.

    President Uhuru signs deals

    In April, the two airlines entered into a mutual partnership to allow them to collaborate in key areas of training for two years.

    As reported by TUKO.co.ke, the signing of the business deal was witnessed by President Kenyatta and his Congolese host, President Tshisekedi in Kinshasa on Thursday, April 22.

    The two airlines said they will partner in areas of mutual interest, including aircraft maintenance, training, and sharing of excess capacity.

    Also included in the two-year agreement is a route and code sharing provision aimed at expanding the carriers' domestic, African and international networks.
