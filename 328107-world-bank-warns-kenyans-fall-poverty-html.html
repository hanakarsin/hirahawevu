    World Bank warns more than half of Kenyans could fall into poverty

    - Word Bank stated more needs to be done to ensure number of poor people is cut in the country - The international lender noted individuals living in counties in north-eastern Kenya were the most affected

    29 Feb 2024, 00:00

    - Word Bank stated more needs to be done to ensure number of poor people is cut in the country

    - The international lender noted individuals living in counties in north-eastern Kenya were the most affected

    - The bank recommended enrolling more people in social protection programmes as one way of curbing poverty

    More than half of Kenyans could fall into poverty in the next two years if urgent measures are not taken by the government, a new report by World Bank has revealed.

    The report said 52% of Kenyans are at risk of falling into poverty if material reforms to eradicate the vice are not implemented.

    The international lender stated that while poverty in the country has reduced significantly from 63% 10 years ago, more needs to be done to ensure the number of poor people is cut.

    One of the recommendations of the report the government needs to adopt to curb poverty is enrolling more people in social protection programmes such as the Inua Jamii cash transfer which benefits poor and vulnerable people.

    The report noted individuals living in counties in north-eastern Kenya were the most affected and were living below the national poverty average.

    In October 2019, the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) stated inflation rose to 4.95% from 3.83% in September 2019 on account of high foodstuff prices.

    The prices of a kilogram of loose grain maize flour, sifted maize flour and tomatoes for instance increased by 5.82, 4.58 and 4.44% respectively.

    In November 2019, the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority increased fuel prices by an average of two shillings while Kenya Power submitted a proposal to increase electricity tariffs by 20%.

    This comes at a time when Kenyans on social media have decried the high cost of living as President Uhuru Kenyatta claims he does not know why Kenyans are broke.
