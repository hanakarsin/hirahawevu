    Former Kiambu governor Ferdinand Waititu has declared he will reclaim the gubernatorial position in the 2022 elections.

    Waititu, who was ousted from the plum seat in 2019 over abuse of office charges, on Sunday, September 19, declared he will run for governor in Kiambu so that he can complete the projects he had started for his constituents.

    Speaking in Githunguri after accompanying Deputy President William Ruto to a church service in the area, the impeached county boss hinted at contesting for the post under a United Democratic Alliance (UDA) ticket.

    At the same time, Babayao, as he is popularly known, also welcomed the reconciliation calls of President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy adding their fallout led to his ejection from office.
