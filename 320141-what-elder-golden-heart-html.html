    The most outstanding Kenyans have the privilege of receiving presidential orders, decorations and medals. These are meant to acknowledge exemplary achievement in various fields and service to the country. Elder of Golden Heart is one of such awards. The awards are conferred by the president after receiving advice from the National Honours and Awards Committee in the president’s office.

    It is not easy for someone to get a state award because it means he or she has to be the very best at what he does. However, in the past few years, Kenyans have questioned how some of the recipients came to receive such awards. Some feel that the awards have been used to reward political sycophants while real heroes of our country are left without recognition. Kenya has no shortage of outstanding men and women who have not been acknowledged for their contribution to the country.

    We have sportsmen who fly the country’s flag high and other people leading the nation in aspects such as innovation and promoting unity among Kenyans. In the just-concluded Mashujaa Day celebrations, some state awards were issued, and one of them was Elder of Golden Heart given to Eliud Kipchoge for being the first man to run a sub marathon in under 2 hours.

    Elder of Golden Heart - EGH meaning in Kenya

    Elder of Golden Heart is a head of state commendation issued to statesmen including clergymen, athletes, journalists, politicians, technocrats and academicians among others. The award is conferred only by a sitting president of the Republic of Kenya. There are eight state awards, and the highest is the Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya.

    Classes of Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya

    This reward is divided into three categories with a seniority order. The categories include:

    Chief of the Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya (C.G.H.)

    The most senior is the Chief of the Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya (C.G.H.). The reward is set aside for presidents. Apart from the Kenyan presidents, the award has also been conferred on Ugandan President Yoweri Museveni, the Agha Khan IV and Liberian Ellen Johnson Sirleaf among others.

    Elder of the Golden Heart of Kenya (E.G.H.)

    The award is conferred on deputy presidents, speakers of parliament, cabinet secretaries, First Ladies, wives of deputy presidents, heads of public service and chief of staff of the Kenya Defense Forces.

    Moran of the Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya (M.G.H.)

    The lowest class of this award is the Moran of the Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya (M.G.H.). The recipients of this prize include the inspector general of police and other senior K.D.F. commanders.

    Other state awards given by the president in order of seniority

    • Order of the Burning Spear
    • Order of the Grand Warrior of Kenya
    • Distinguished Conduct Order
    • Distinguished Service Medal
    • Silver Star of Kenya
    • Head of State’s Commendation
    • Uhodari Medal

    The criterion for Award of the Elder of Golden Heart

    A deserving individual is nominated for the prize by district committees, government ministries, religious entities, non-governmental sector, individuals and others. The National Honours Act states that those who merit the decoration should be people who display exemplary qualities, achievements of heroism, patriotism or leadership. It must be evident that the individual has made a significant and excellent contribution to the country in journalism, economic, academic, scientific, business, security and many other fields.

    The president confers the honour after taking into account the recommendations from any of three committees. The first is the National and County Governments Honours Advisory Committee. The committee was established to identify the state or public officers serving in the national government or county government and worthy of the honour.

    The second committee that can recommend individuals to be awarded Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya by the president is the Parliamentary Honours Advisory Committee. The mandate of the committee is to identify and recommend individuals including members of parliament, former members of parliament, public officers serving in the parliamentary service or public/state officers serving in an independent commission or office.

    Lastly, the Judiciary Honours Advisory Committee also recommends individuals for state prizes. The committee focuses on judges, judicial officers, former judges, former judicial officers or any other members of the staff of the judiciary.

    Where to find names of recipients of the award of Elder of the Golden Heart of Kenya

    The names of state award recipients are found in the register of national honours. The record is kept and maintained by the cabinet secretary responsible for national heritage. The registry is to be made available for inspection by members of the public. Furthermore, the president may be advised by the Advisory Committee to amend, suspend or even revoke a national honour.     Privileges associated with the award of Elder of the Golden Heart of Kenya

    Individuals who have been awarded a state honour are heroes and heroines, and for that reason, they ought to receive some privileges. There are many benefits associated with the reward such as healthcare services, housing, social security, education and employment. The highly sought privileges include:

    • Financial assistance from the National Heroes Fund in the circumstances that those individuals find themselves in economic situations that warrant such help.
    • Publication of books to depict the role played by prize holder in the history of the country or the social life of the society, thus forming part of the curriculum of education.
    • The issuance of things such as postage stamps, scarves, utensils, souvenirs, apparel and artefacts subject to the determination of the Council.
    • Prizes of medals, commendations, insignia, and certificates among other commemorative things as recommended by the Heroes Council.
    • The naming of things such as roads, towns, institutions, estates, and parks after prize holders.
    • Organizing concerts, cultural festivals, sports events, and exhibitions in honour of the heroes and heroines.
    • Being invited to national and other community functions as special state guests.
