    How well do you know the agencies in your country? Come to think of it, not so many people can tell what some government agencies and regulatory bodies do. One such body whose role remains unknown to many Kenyans is the Capital Market Authority (CMA). CMA is an independent public agency entrenched in Cap 485 A of the constitution and is established by an Act of parliament. It is an arm of the National Treasury and Planning and is meant to supervise, license and monitor capital market activities.

    Capital Markets Authority is the national body charged with the responsibility of regulating and supervising the capital markets industry in Kenya. Formed on December 15, 1989, the agency has been functional under the treasury to oversee activities in the market including the central depository and settlement system, the stock exchange, and all other bodies and persons who are registered under the capital markets in the country.

    One of the core mandates is especially in providing regulation for the financial services by offering strategic guidelines on the number of resources to channel into different sectors. These include government bonds, equities, and other government-related securities in different ranges.

    CMA mandate

    What is the role of Capital Market Authority? The authority’s regulatory functions include:

    • Supervising and licensing all the capital markets in the country as well as their intermediaries.
    • Ensuring that all licensed persons and firms conduct themselves in the acceptable code of behaviour.
    • Regulating and controlling the issuance of capital market products such as shares and bonds.
    • Researching new products towards promoting market development.
    • Promoting public awareness and investor education.
    • Protecting the interests of investors.

    Understanding the obligations that the body is charged to do under the constitution clarifies the responsibilities of Capital Market Authority. It is of paramount importance that you as a citizen of Kenya are familiar with these roles since in one way you could need a service under their jurisdiction.

    How CMA regulates the capital markets in Kenya

    Would you like to know how capital markets are regulated in Kenya? Well, just like any other constitutional body that is charged with a mandate to deliver some responsibilities, CMA conducts its regulatory activities through a regulatory framework that’s crafted within the confines and provisions of the constitution.

    The authority through its board of directors and supported by the management and the chief executive undertakes all the roles as charged by the constitution of Kenya.

    Enabling legislation

    Since its inception, the authority has operated within the provided constitutional framework and guides all stakeholders on compliance. The activities of the body are reflected in the way it conducts its research, develops a regulatory process and creates a level playing field where all players including institutions and other key parties work in fairness and orderliness. The authority regulatory framework comprises of:

    • Capital markets regulations 2001 (for collective investment schemes)

    3. Guidelines

    The authority has developed guidelines that are used for the supervision and management of capital markets and the intermediaries involved. Here are some of the guidelines:

    • Guidelines to regulate corporate governance practices of Public listed companies
    • Directives in the registration and approval of credit rating companies in the country
    • Guidelines for regulating intermediaries and their financial requirements

    These regulations and the rules set for markets are practical and ensure maximum practicality in execution. They are meant to provide smooth supervision and management of capital stocks towards an economically sound country.

    Licensees of the CMA

    Licensees refer to the bodies and agencies that rely on the CMA regulations and provisions to conduct business in Kenya. The authority licenses the following categories of markets players:

    • Securities exchanges
    • Central depositories
    • Investment banks
    • Stockbrokers
    • Dealers
    • Investment advisers
    • Fund managers
    • Authorized securities dealers
    • Authorized depositories (Custodians)
    • Credit rating agencies
    • Venture capital companies

    The role of Capital Market Authority in economic development in Kenya is not limited to only regulation and licensing. Without the authority, the market would be imbalanced as any player would set the rules and do a hostile takeover.

    With the body in place, one can be sure of the regulations and guidelines that govern how Nairobi stocks exchange operates. From the licensing, the government earns revenue which in turn is returned in the form of employing regulators and developing the necessary infrastructure to support capital market activities.

    Composition of Capital Market Authority

    The authority has its members who ensure its operations are successful. They consist of:

    • A chairman appointed by the president. He can also be recommended by the finance minister.
    • Six other appointees appointed by the authority of the finance minister.
    • The finance ministry permanent secretary or a member that deputizes him/her for the act.
    • Governor of the Central Bank of Kenya or any other person who deputizes him/her.
    • The attorney general or his/her deputy
    • The chief executive of CMA

    Objectives of CMA

    The objectives of CMA as provided for in the constitution are as follows:

    • Removing impediments that deter long-term investments by country’s productive enterprises and companies.
    • Development of market aspects necessary for continuity.
    • Facilitating the nationwide system of the stock market and brokerage services to facilitate more public participation in the stock market.
