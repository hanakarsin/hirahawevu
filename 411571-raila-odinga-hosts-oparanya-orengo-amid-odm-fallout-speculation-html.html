    Raila Odinga Hosts Oparanya, Orengo Amid ODM Fallout Speculation

    - The two leaders met Raila amid speculation that they were planning a walkout from the ODM party - Oparanya and Orengo reportedly met on Sunday, leaving their supporters in a dilemma given they did not brief anyone on their meeting

    - Their meeting with Raila has been seen as an antidote to deflating the rising tension among their supporters

    In a bid to disapprove the naysayers, perceived ODM rebels reunited with the party leader Raila Odinga at his Karen home on Monday, May 10.

    This happened as murmurs came about that ODM had been rocked by internal wrangles pitting two factions within the party.

    Siaya senator James Orengo and Kakamega governor Wycliffe Oparanya were reported to have met on Sunday, May 9, to deliberate on their position regarding the matters that had happened to be the bone of contention in the party.

    Rarieda MP Otiende Amollo was also present during the said meeting.

    Oparanya has recently been seen to dissent from the orange party leader, often hanging out with Deputy President William Ruto, bringing forth speculation.

    Members within the party had been divided on the BBI bill, with one faction pushing for certain amendments on it, the other vouching for the bill to be passed in its entirety by the bicameral parliament.

    The faction pushing for the amendments was led by Siaya senator James Orengo who sided with Rarieda MP Otiende Amollo, taking on the other wing, led by John Mbadii and Junet Mohammed, that stood with the party's position to have the bill passed without any changes.

    The arguments saw the ousting of Otiende Amollo as the vice-chair of the National Assembly Justice and Legal Affairs Committee (JLAC), with rumours getting rife that Orengo was also slated for an ouster as the Senate minority leader.

    The leaders meeting with Raila has been seen as a move to deflate the tension among ODM supporters and other concerned parties.

    Shortly after meeting Raila, the three leaders moved to Kajiado, where they caught up with COTU Secretary General Francis Atwoli at his home.

    While they said the meeting with Atwoli was a mere buddies' hangout, political eyes spotted otherwise, given the COTU boss has played host to the who-is-who in the country's political space.

    Whenever Atwoli hosted different political bigwigs, assertions came up that they were strategising for 2022.

    Other leaders ever hosted at Atwoli's home are Jubilee vice-chair David Murathe, former presidential hopeful Peter Kenneth, KANU leader Gideon Moi and ODM leader Raila Odinga.
