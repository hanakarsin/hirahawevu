    The Wiper party, led by Kalonzo Musyoka, has said it will discipline five ward representatives who visited Deputy President William Ruto at his Karen residence.

    The MCAs, Tariq Mulatya (Masinga Central), Cosmas Ngula (Matungulu East), Cosmus Kieti (Lower Kaewa), Joseph Wambua (Mbiuni), and Agatha Mutunga (nominated), were accused of flouting the Political Parties Act.

    Machakos County Assembly Speaker Florence Mwangangi said the Opposition outfit would take action against the county assembly members, The Standard reported.

    "The code of conduct of political leaders is spelt out in the Political Parties Act regarding their relationship with other political parties.

    "Their actions clearly contravened the established guidelines and principles set out in law and they must be ready to face the consequences," said Mwangangi.

    The speaker warned that Wiper's disciplinary organ wouldn't spare the MCAs.

    Mwangangi and Leader of Majority Mark Muendo stated that by visiting Ruto who is associated with the United Democratic Alliance (UDA), the ward reps went against the party that sponsored them in the 2017 General Election.

    "They (MCAs) cannot argue that they were paying a courtesy call to the DP as such is done formally and procedurally," Mwangangi added.

    Jubilee suspends MCAs

    In November 2020, the ruling Jubilee Party suspended three nominated Nairobi County Assembly members for disregarding the role of the party in various assembly matters.

    The three, Ann Mumbi, Silvia Kihoro and Millicent Jagero, were suspended for three months and excluded from the assembly's committees.
