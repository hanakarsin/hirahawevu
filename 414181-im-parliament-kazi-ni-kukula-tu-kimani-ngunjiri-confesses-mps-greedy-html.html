    Controversial Bahati MP Kimani Ngunjiri has accused Kenyan political leadership of greed and hypocrisy.

    Ngunjiri said the Kenyan parliament lost its meaning long ago, and no MP is currently serving the interest of the common mwananchi but the highest political bidders and state operatives.

    Speaking to Hot 96 on Wednesday, June 2, the Nakuru Tanga Tanga playmaker lamented that his colleagues in both House are easily bribed to pass bills, even those they do not believe in.

    "I'm in parliament, I will tell you for free, ni kukula na kukula tu. Money is just moving between hands. Hakuna mtu ako na shughuli ya Wanjiku(The parliament is about receiving bribes, no one is interested in Wanjiku anymore)," he claimed.

    The first time lawmaker also took an issue with the National Prayer Breakfast hosted by the National Assembly and the Senate Speakers at the Parliament Building on Thursday, May 27.

    "I can never attend the National Prayer Day. I know how those MPs behave in parliament. They now go and pretend in front of the screens. It's like a hyena calling for a meeting of goats and sheep," he added.

    Ngunjiri's allegations support similar claims made by his Gatundu South colleague Moses Kuria.

    On May 7, Kuria claimed legislators in the National Assembly were bribed with KSh 100,000 to vote for the 2020 Constitutional Amendment Bill.

    The Jubilee politician who opposed the BBI Bill on the floor of the House alleged the 224 MPs who endorsed the document were paid by a senior parliamentary leader whom he did not name.

    "MPs voting yes currently receiving their KSh 100,000 from the office of a senior parliamentary leader," Kuria posted on Facebook.
