    Joyce Syombua's mother says she warned her against visiting estranged husband in Nanyuki

    - On Saturday, November 16, police recovered three bodies from Makaburini are in Laikipia county - The bodies are suspected to be Joyce Symboua and her two children Shanice and Michael - They went missing after visiting their father Peter Mugure, a KDF officer at Nanyuki barracks

    12 Feb 2024, 00:00

    - On Saturday, November 16, police recovered three bodies from Makaburini are in Laikipia county

    - The bodies are suspected to be Joyce Symboua and her two children Shanice and Michael

    - They went missing after visiting their father Peter Mugure, a KDF officer at Nanyuki barracks

    - Prior to the visitation, the mother had won a child upkeep case against her husband

    - Syombua's mother says she had warned her daughter against the visit but she did not listen to her

    Mother to Joyce Syombua who went missing with her two children says she had warned her daughter against visiting estranged husband Peter Mugure at Nanyuki barracks.

    Elizabeth Maua Malombe said she listened to her intuition, which communicated to her that something bad was going to happen to her daughter and grandchildren should they visit Mugure in Laikipia county.

    Speaking to Citizen TV on Saturday, November 17, Malombe disclosed that a week to October 25, when Syombua left their home in Kayole, Nairobi, with her two children Shanice Maua and Prince Michael, she had won a child upkeep case against her husband.

    “Syombua had sued Mugure for child upkeep after he said he couldn’t support the children because he was not sure that the kids were sired by him. "One week to October 25, the judge ruled that Mugure should provide for the children after DNA test results on the kids revealed that he was their biological father,” said Malombe.

    READ ALSO: Ngina Kenyatta advises youth on how to beat unemployment

    However, Mugure was granted regular access to his children by the court and that is what led to Syombua and the children visiting him at Nanyuki barracks.

    “I, however, warned her that my intuition was not for the visit, and that Mugure should come to Kayole, Nairobi and see the children; that was on October 24. "My daughter, however, told me that nothing bad would happen to them, and that Mugure had even sent her bus-fare to visit him in Nanyuki. I told her: ‘Okay, go ahead with your plan, but know that I am against it,” said Malombe.

    READ ALSO: Isuzu yamtuza Eliud Kipchoge gari lenye thamani ya KSh4.1 milioni

    The mother's sentiments came after police recovered three bodies from Makaburini in Thingithu area with the children stacked on top of their mother suspected to be those of the missing three.

    Thingithu is approximately 9-minutes away from the Laikipia Air Base.

    Mugure was arrested on Friday, November 15, following a report that was filed by Malombe at Kayole Police Station who reported she was unable to reach her daughter on phone.

    Syombua's friend identified as Farizana had also reported the matter at Nanyuki Police Station.

    Mugure was arrested as the main suspect and questioned by detectives for over seven hours.

    The Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) officers told police she had escorted her wife to Nayuki bus station and the boarded a Nairobi bound matatu but he was not sure if they left.

    A cellphone belonging to the 31-year-old mother would later be discovered inside one of the matatus plying Nanyuki-Nyahururu road and not Nyanyuki-Nairobi highway.

    The KDF officer is still held at Nanyuki Police Station awaiting arraignment in court.
