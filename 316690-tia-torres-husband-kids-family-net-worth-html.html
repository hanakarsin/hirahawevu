    Tia Torres bio

    Looking to know more about the early life of Tia Torres? Here is a look at her biography:

    Nationality and birthplace

    Tia Torres was born on 11th June 1960 as Tia Maria Torres, making her 59 years old. She was born in Southern California, in California, U.S.A. She is, therefore, a US citizen.

    Early life

    Tia was born to a middle-class family, but her childhood was anything but smooth. Her parents who are unnamed, divorced and her father married another woman.

    On top of their broken home, her mother later died when she was still very young, living her to be raised by her step-mother. According to Tia, she was her role model and the person she considered to be her parental figure.

    Education background and passion for animals

    There is not much that is known about Tia’s education background including which schools she attended. However, they are probably in Southern California, where she spent her childhood. What is known though about Tia Torres while younger is that she was very fond of animals from a very young age –a passion she attributes to her bond with her stepmother.

Over time, she developed a huge interest in the welfare of animals, going from more than just caring for horses to taking in strays.     Tia Torres family

    Looking to know more about the personal life of Tia Maria Torres? Below is a look at her personal relationships.

    Parents

    There is not much information about Tia’s parents, with her father, biological mother, and step mother’s names not being mentioned. However, she has credited her stepmother for playing a huge role in her growing up.

    It was her stepmother's ideology that people should keep pets and treat them well that led to her developing a passion for animals. She has also claimed that her stepmother was a strict disciplinarian while she was growing up and that she developed a very close bond with her.

    Husband

    Tia Torres is currently married to Aren Marcus Jackson, whom she met in the 1980s when she was searching for the owner of a dog – who turned out to be Jackson. They would later reconnect in 2001, and at the time, he was serving a sentence stemming from his involvement in a shootout with the police. They became pen pals, and when he was released from prison, the couple tied the knot.

    Tia Torres career

    The animal rescuer has worn many hats over the last few decades. Here are the most notable jobs she has undertaken so far:

    Military

    Was Tia Torres in the military? Yes! The violence of the gang life led her to want out, and it was during this time that she enlisted for the military. She posted high scores on her army entrance exams, and the recruiters recommended that she follow a medical path. However, she opted for a different course, and ended up attending a truck driving school and becoming an army truck driver for infantry troops.

    However, she would not last in the military forever. After 6 years of serving her country, she decided to leave the army, returning to Los Angeles where she became a youth gang counsellor.

    Youth gang counsellor

    Tia Torres was invited by Los Angeles City to be part of a programme aimed at young gang members, which was designed to help solve their issues peacefully without resorting to violence. Under the programme, she would go into gang neighbourhoods and talk with gang members and their parents. She would also offer counselling to members, to help them with among other issues, stay in school.

    Animal rescue business

    After leaving the army, Tia came across a pit bull in an animal shelter, who she adopted and named Tanaka. Tanaka became an integral part of her life, including her family. It was through her that she developed a love for the breed, which led to her founding Villalobos (meaning “wolves village” in Spanish) Rescue Centre.

    This centre would grow to become one of the largest put bulls rescue facility in the U.S. As part of the rescue business, she runs training seminars, medical services, and obedience classes for pit bulls.

    Pit Bulls and Parolees

    Tia’s pit bulls rescue centre is non-profit, something that saw her struggling with finances to fund the programme. This was the main reason that led her to become a TV Personality. Her famous TV show Pit Bulls & Parolees started airing in 2009 on Animal Planet network, and it follows her animal rescue work under the Villalobos Rescue Centre. The Pit Bulls and Parolees cast include Tia, her children, and parolees, who help her to rescue and rehabilitate pit bulls across the country.

    How much does Tia pay the parolees?

    It is not known how much Tia Torres pays her parolees. Funds to cover their pay and other expenses come from adoption fees and donations from well-wishers. However, after becoming part of the Pit Bulls & Parolees programme, Animal Planet helped to pay for some of the rescue centre’s expenses, which at the time the show was starting were at $25,000.

    Did Pit Bulls and Parolees get cancelled?

    No! The show is still on-going and it is set to return later in 2019. However, there has been a lot of controversy with her programme, with most critics not being fond of Tia Torres' dog training methods and advice.

    The controversy stems from critics who believe that pit bulls are too dangerous a dog, something that the show does not emphasize on. As a result, there has been a lot of campaigns to remove the Pit Bulls & Parolees show from the air.
