    Nairobi County Assembly has approved a motion that will see Uhuru Park and Central Park closed for three months ahead of scheduled renovation works.

    On Wednesday, September 28, Members of the County Assembly unanimously passed the motion so that the two parks at the heart of the city get a facelift given their poor state.

    Majority leader Abdi Guyo, who was the mover of the motion, called for immediate closure of the parks to allow restoration to their former glory.

    Guyo said plans to refurbish the parks came up following consultations with the Nairobi Metropolitan Services (NMS) and City Hall.

    Notably, the county government is charged with taking care of parks and green spaces such as Uhuru Park, Central Park, Michuki Memorial Park, Jamhuri Park, Jeevanjee Gardens, and Uhuru Gardens, among others.

    The MCAs called on the NMS Director-General Mohamed Badi and Nairobi acting governor Ann Kananu to notify the public and subsequently order for the closure of the two parks to allow for renovations.

    Guyo regretted that the parks were neglected for years saying they were at risk of losing their appeal and historical significance if actions will not be taken as soon as possible.

    “While NMS has invested heavily in the renovation of Michuki Memorial Park, the same has not materialised for Uhuru Park and Central Park which remain in a state of disrepair,” said Guyo.

    He explained that rehabilitation and retrofitting of urban parks and green spaces in Nairobi is part of the presidential legacy projects and therefore needs the full support of the county.

    Guyo was seconded by minority leader Michael Ogada who suggested that the renovations would be undertaken for approximately three months to ensure that good work was done.

    “Let us give them the three months they are requesting for but let them not fence off the place and then disappear,” stated Ogada.

    Dandora Area 4 MCA Francis Ngesa told the MCAs to ensure that no single inch of public land goes to private hands.

    “We must assure the public that there will be nothing sinister going to happen in the parks. We must also ensure that not a single inch of public land goes to private hands,” Ngesa said.

    Mowlem MCA Benson Mwangi regretted that Uhuru park had been turned into a den of thieves and hangout area for drug abusers.

    Uhuru Park is a 12.9-hectare recreational park adjacent to the central business district of Nairobi.
