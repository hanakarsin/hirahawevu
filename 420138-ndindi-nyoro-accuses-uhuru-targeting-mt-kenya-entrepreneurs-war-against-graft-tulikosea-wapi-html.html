    Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro has linked the tribulations facing his Mathira counterpart Rigathi Gachagua to a political witch-hunt allegedly orchestrated by President Uhuru Kenyatta.

    Gachagua was the guest of state the whole weekend before being released on Monday, July 26, after pleading not guilty to fraud and money laundering charges.

    Speaking after his release, Nyoro accused the head of state of targeting Mount Kenya entrepreneurs in the war against corruption.

    "All this is a political witch hunt, we elected you (Uhuru) and after you got the position, you are now paying us back through frustration and intimidation. Where did we wrong you? posed Nyoro.

    The lawmaker added that the majority of leaders from Central Kenya that were being targeted were those allied to Deputy President William Ruto.

    He claimed that the moment a politician is seen with the second in command, the state starts to investigate him unearthing unsubstantiated claims.

    KEMSA scandal

    The Tanga Tanga allied lawmaker questioned why only Gachagua was being pursued when those implicated in the multi-billion Kenya Medical Supplies Authority (KEMSA) scandal were still at large.

    He claimed that the 'untouchables' in the government were covering the scandal after realising their friends and families had also benefited from it.

    Nyoro challenged the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss George Kinoti to also arrest the KEMSA scandal beneficiaries christened COVID-19 billionaires just like he did to Gachagua.

    He, together with other 'hustlers' from the mountain vowed to stay put insisting intimidation will not sway them from supporting Ruto's 2022 bid.

    "We will not be intimidated by cerelac and sausage kids, sisi ni watu tulikula nduma (we were fed arrow roots while growing up) we came to Nairobi to secure the future of our children," said Nyoro.

    As previously reported, Gachagua faced several charges relating to money laundering and other economic crimes in which he was accused of unlawfully receiving KSh 7 billion.

    Appearing before chief magistrate Lawrence Mugambi on Monday, July 26, Gachagua maintained he is an innocent man and was released on a cash bail of KSh 12 million or KSh 25 million bond.
