    African women hairstyles- origin

    Hair played a very significant role in the ancient African culture, and it was a symbol of one’s family background, marital status, social class, tribe, and spirituality. Here is the history of some of the most popular African hairstyles;

    Braids and beads from the Wodaabe tribe of West Africa and Sahel region

    The Wodaabe tribe are a pastoral nomadic tribe with a population of around 100,000. The girls and women of the tribe wear braided hairstyles that are similar to the Fulani women. The hairstyle has two braids on either side or a coiffure in the middle.

    Ochre dreadlocks of Hamar tribe in Ethiopia

    The Hamar Tribe is a pastoral community that has an estimated population of twenty thousand people who live in the Omo Valley of Ethiopia. The women of Hamar tribe adorned often with colorful beaded jewelry, and they like to wear their hair in thin ochre dreadlocks created with water and binding risen. The adolescent girls of the tribe have their hair twisted into cornrows and decorated with beads.
