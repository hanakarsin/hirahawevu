    What are Saccos?

    Saccos are a type of savings and credit cooperative organization commonly known as a credit union. These organizations are formed by members who pool their financial resources together, allowing them to access credit and other financial services at lower costs than those offered by traditional banks. Saccos are a great way to save money and gain access to credit while also supporting local communities.

    What are the factors to consider while choosing a Sacco in Kenya?

    Choosing a Sacco in Kenya requires careful consideration of several factors such as:

    • Reputation and stability: Look for a Sacco with a solid reputation for financial stability and integrity. Research its history, track record, and any news or reviews about its services.
    • Regulation and licensing: Ensure the Sacco is registered and regulated by the relevant authority in Kenya, such as the Sacco Societies Regulatory Authority (SASRA). This ensures compliance with legal and regulatory standards, providing a level of security for your savings.
    • Membership requirements: Find out if you qualify for membership based on your profession, location, or other criteria.
    • Membership fees: Ask about any membership fees or minimum share capital required to join.
    • Dividends and rebates: See if the Sacco offers dividends on your savings or rebates on loan interest.
    • Online and mobile banking: If you prefer online banking, check if the Sacco offers a user-friendly platform.
    • Customer reviews and reputation: Talk to friends, family, or colleagues who are members of Saccos. Read online reviews to get a sense of the Sacco's reputation for customer service and efficiency.

    How do Kenyan Saccos work?

    Kenyan Saccos are financial institutions that provide members with access to savings and loan products. They are owned and operated by their members, who are usually people from the same community, and are often organized around a common purpose or interest.

    Members make regular deposits into the Sacco and then can use the pooled funds to take out loans for personal or business needs. The loans are usually provided at low-interest rates, and Sacco also often provides other services such as life insurance and retirement plans.

    How much interest do saccos pay in Kenya?

    Saccos in Kenya are regulated by the Sacco Societies Regulatory Authority (SASRA), which safeguards member interests by establishing limitations in interest rates. Rates may vary depending on the type of loan or accounts. Here are a few guidelines:

    • Saccos offer interest rates on saving accounts ranging from around 5% to 10% per annum. Some offer loans based on account balances. The higher the balance the higher the rates.
    • For fixed deposit account, the rates are slightly higher compared to savings account. rates range from 8% to 12% per annum or higher depending on the duration.
