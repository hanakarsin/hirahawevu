    The 'DWTS' Season 30 Cast Includes a 'Bachelor' and a Controversial YouTuber

    By Shannon Raphael

    Updated Sept. 8 2021, 10:29 a.m. ET

    Though many mourn the end of the summer, the beginning of fall marks the return of beloved TV programs. The hit dancing competition series, Dancing with the Stars, is set to return to the small screen on Sept. 20 for a landmark 30th season. While host Tyra Banks, the judging panel, and most of the pros will look familiar from Season 29, the Season 30 cast will be making history.

    ABC revealed the full list of celebrities who will be donning their dancing shoes during the Sept. 8 broadcast of GMA. Keep reading to find out everything we know about Season 30.

    The 'DWTS' Season 30 cast features an Olympian, a Bravolebrity, and several notable influencers.

    Ahead of the Good Morning America announcement, ABC did confirm two cast members. Olympic gold medalist Sunisa "Suni" Lee will swap out her gymnastics leotard for a glittery DWTS costume on Season 30.

    Suni won three medals at the 2020 Tokyo Olympic Games, and she's joining the competition while also navigating her freshman year of college at Auburn University.

    The athlete isn't the only teenager who will be on the show. YouTube personality JoJo Siwa will make history on the series as the first person to compete with a same-sex partner. The Dance Moms alum (who clearly has a leg up on the competition with her background) is rumored to be working with Jenna Johnson Chmerkovskiy.

    During GMA, the remaining contestants vying for the Mirror Ball trophy were revealed. The Real Housewives of Atlanta star Kenya Moore and The Bachelor Season 25 lead Matt James will be dancing.

    Fitness instructor and The Talk co-host Amanda Kloots is also one of the cast members. Many were introduced to the Broadway dancer when her husband, Nick Cordero, passed away from COVID-19 in 2020.

    Brian Austin Green is another celebrity who is joining Season 30. The Beverly Hills, 90210 actor has appeared on a slew of reality contests in recent years, and his partner will reportedly be his girlfriend/DWTS pro, Sharna Burgess.

    Olivia Jade Giannulli, the eldest daughter of Lori Loughlin and Mossimo Giannulli, is participating on the series as well. While some knew of Olivia from her YouTube career and her beauty collaborations, a lot of people first learned about her when her parents were involved in the 2019 college admissions scandal.

    Musicians Melanie C and Jimmie Allen will participate, as will actors Melora Hardin and Martin Kove.

    The two athletes on Season 30 are Iman Shumpert and Mike "The Miz" Mizanin. Both also star on reality shows about their families.

    Another reality personality who will be competing is Bling Empire cast member Christine Chiu.

    Those who purchased Peloton bikes during the COVID-19 pandemic will recognize the final celebrity dancer. Instructor Cody Rigsby will swap out his cycling shoes for dancing footwear.

    The pros, the host, and the judges have all been revealed for 'DWTS' Season 30.

    The celebrity cast members often get the most attention from season to season, but their professional partners are just as important when it comes to their chances of winning.

    The Season 30 male pros include Valentin Chmerkovskiy, Gleb Savenchko, Alan Bersten, Brandon Armstrong, Sasha Farber, Pasha Pashkov, and Artem Chigvintsev.

    The female professionals are Witney Carson, Emma Slater, Britt Stewart, Daniella Karagach, Cheryl Burke, Jenna Johnson Chmerkovskiy, Sharna Burgess, and Lindsay Arnold.

    The pros and their partners will be dancing for Bruno Tonioli, Carrie Ann Inaba, and Derek Hough. Len Goodman, who had to step aside during Season 29 because of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, will be back. Tyra Banks will return for her second season as the host. Dancing with the Stars Season 30 will premiere on Sept. 20 at 8 p.m. EST on ABC.
