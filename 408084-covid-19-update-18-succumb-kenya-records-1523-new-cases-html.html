    Some 1,523 more people have tested positive for the dreaded coronavirus from a sample size of 7,423 tested in the last 24 hours, the Ministry of Health has announced.

    Kenya's positivity rate stood at 20.5% as of Wednesday, April 7. The Ministry of Health said the country had recorded a total of 141,365 positive cases so far. The cumulative tests stood at 1,530,736.

    In terms of distribution of the virus in the counties, Nairobi recorded 626, Kiambu 142, Uasin Gishu 108, Nakuru 92, Mombasa 65, Kericho 46, Murang’a 45, Kisumu 36, Kilifi 36, Machakos 36, Turkana 34, Nandi 30, Makueni 29, Kajiado 27, Meru 19, Kakamega 17, Taita Taveta 15, Nyandarua 14, Embu 13, Busia 12, Nyeri 11.

    Garissa had 10, Laikipia 10, Siaya 9, Kitui 8, Kisii 7, Baringo 3, Kwale 3, Mandera 3, Nyamira 3, Trans Nzoia 3, Kirinyaga 2, Vihiga 2, Narok 1, Tharaka Nithi 1, West Pokot 1, Bungoma 1, Elgeyo Marakwet 1, Homa Bay 1 and Isiolo 1.

    "616 patients have recovered from the disease, 522 are from Home Based and Isolation Care, while 94 are from various health facilities. Total recoveries now stand at 97,194," MoH said in a statement.

    The health ministry also reported 18 more deaths, all of them being late death reports from facility record audits that occurred on diverse dates. This pushed the cumulative fatalities to 2,276.

    The ministry further reported that Nairobi county contributed 33% of the total number of people vaccinated so far followed by Nakuru and Uasin Gishu counties at 7% and 6% respectively.

    "Lamu county with an uptake of 248 persons vaccinated has consistently remained lower since the exercise started," read a statement from MoH.

    The Mutahi Kagwe led-ministry said that 228 people had also been vaccinated with the Sputnik vaccine bringing the total number of those vaccinated to 340,121.

    As of Tuesday, April 6, a total of 339,893 people had been vaccinated with the Astrazeneca vaccine; 99,084 health workers, 27,945 security officers, 45,877 teachers and 166,987 other members of the public who include those at 58 years and above.

    Meanwhile, Health Principal Secretary Susan Mochache has received a donation of COVID-19 testing Kits from the German Government and the Aga Khan University Hospital towards combating COVID-19 at Kenya Medical Research Institute.

    The 20,000 testing kits valued at KSh 44 million were presented by the German ambassador to Kenya Annett Gunter and AKUN Interim CEO Rashid Khalani.

    “This consignment will go a long way in supporting the Kenya government’s response to the pandemic,” the PS said.

    The Kenyan government had admitted that one of its critical components in the fight against the virus was the oxygen supply to patients.

    Availability of oxygen remained low in public health facilities at 16%, and where available the supply was not optimal and lacksd the necessary distribution and delivery infrastructure.

    "Aware that oxygen was going to be a critical factor in our COVID-19 response, we set up an oxygen task team from the deliberations of a COVID-19 taskforce committee meeting in the month of July 2020," said CS Kagwe.

    The objectives of the task team were among others to review the status of oxygen supply in the country, particularly in designated COVID-19 national and county facilities.
