    Raila Odinga hosts Laikipia woman rep and Ruto ally Cate Waruguru

    - Raila Odinga said they used the opportunity to discuss national issues including change of the constitution - Waruguru on her part noted there was a need for national unity to end tribalism in the country

    11 May 2024, 00:00

    - Raila Odinga said they used the opportunity to discuss national issues including change of the constitution

    - Waruguru on her part noted there was a need for national unity to end tribalism in the country

    - The former nominated MCA said she had no problem working with all politicians so long as her Laikipia residents would benefit

    - The woman rep is a strong supporter of DP Ruto and the Tanga Tanga wing of the Jubilee Party

    The Orange Democratic Movement leader Raila Odinga has played host to Laikipia Woman Representative Cate Waruguru.

    The politician was accompanied by the ODM minority whip in the National Assembly Junet Mohamed to the mid-afternoon meeting on Tuesday, June 9.

    Though no much details were given concerning the surprise courtesy call, Raila confirmed they engaged on critical issues affecting the country at the moment.

    "I hosted Laikipia county MP Catherine Waruguru, who was accompanied by Minority Whip Junet Mohamed. We held a discussion on critical national issues including constitutional reforms and national unity," he said.

    On her part, the former nominated MCA said there was a need for national unity and end of tribalism once and for all for the sake of peace and independence.

    According to her, she did not mind working with all politicians so long as her Laikipia residents would stand to benefit.

    "And as long as my people in Laikipia will benefit from government projects, I don't mind. They are the ones that matter to me," he posted.

    This was the second time in barely two weeks the leader was hosting a politician from the ruling Jubilee Party after a similar one on Saturday, May 23.

    "I held a meeting with a section of MPs from the Jubilee Party and exchanged notes on the state of affairs in the country," he confirmed via social media.

    The development came at a time the President Uhuru Kenyatta-led outfit was cleaning its house and punishing those it perceived as rebels in the Tanga Tanga wing.

    Waruguru and her Tanga Tanga partners in crime openly criticised and opposed Uhuru's order directing members to stop pre-mature 2022 campaigns.

    Before the government's ban on social gatherings, the MPs under the tutelage of Deputy President William Ruto criss-crossed the country under the guise of launching projects.

    On June 2, the party removed Tanga Tanga MP Cecily Mbarire, the deputy majority whip, alongside majority whip Benjamin Washiali and replaced them with MPs Maoka Maore and Emmanuel Wangwe respectively.
