    What is the most beautiful country in the world? Since beauty is a subjective term and is a hard thing to quantify, United Nations agencies like UNESCO organizations like the World Economic Forum help us discover the most beautiful countries in the world. They use various factors like the natural resources, architectural designs of modern and ancient structures, vegetation, wildlife, and landscapes to determine a country's beauty.

    Before UNESCO and the World Economic Forum enlists a nation as one of the prettiest countries in the world, they consider factors like safety, life expectancy, conservation of natural resources, and how eye-catching the natural sites are. Most people use these reports to find the most beautiful places and natural areas to visit while planning vacations.

    The world has many beautiful countries to visit before you die. Touring some of these spectacular tourist destinations won't leave you broke. Some of them might be in your country. Below are 15 interesting countries with some of the most beautiful locations in the world.

    1. Brazil

    Brazil is the most attractive country. Everything about it, from Rio De Jadinero to the countryside and the sandy beaches, is astounding. It is also the longest globally, spanning 4,395 km (2,731 mi) from north to south.

    Amazon and other forests have precious vegetation and animals, including the jaguars, piranhas, giant water lilies, and mangroves. Besides the Amazon, Brazil has everything tourists want to see in South America.

    Fernando de Noronha, an archipelago off the northeast coast, and the peak of Corcovado are only a fraction of the Brazilian appeal. Furthermore, Lençóis Maranhenses National Park is a sight to behold. It has uber-white dunes juxtaposed against turquoise lagoons.

    2. Canada

    Canada is famously known as the world's second-largest country by total area and America's UBER-friendly neighbor. It is in North America and has about 38.19 million people. Queen Victoria chose Ottawa as Canada's capital in 1857.

    The country covers 9.98 million square kilometers from the Atlantic, Pacific to the Arctic Ocean. Canada ranks so high on this list because it has so many beautiful places to offer.

    The snowy peaks and maple syrup are a beauty to behold. The mesmerizing landscape of the lake-filled Banff National Park in the Rocky Mountains will tempt you not to return home on time. You must see the breathtaking Niagara Falls.

    3. Costa Rica

    You will traverse Costa Rica, a rugged, rain-forested Central American country with coastlines on the Caribbean and Pacific, and never run out of natural vegetation. Almost three-quarters of the country is covered by nature.

    Costa Rica also has deserted beaches and gorgeous jungles. Cocos Island National Park is the only island in the eastern Pacific with a rainforest. Here, you will find hammerhead sharks, giant manta rays, sea turtles, and dolphins.

    Also, the misty waterfalls, beautiful landscape, glacial valleys, a rare tropical alpine grassland, and the forests are a breath of fresh air. Additionally, Costa Rica's capital city, San José, has an underground museum called the Pre-Columbian Gold Museum.

    4. England

    England's beauty is to die for, and there is no shortage of cliché-ridden ideas about this country. For instance, visitors are often surprised to find that it doesn't rain as much as they had heard, and the Indian restaurants outnumber fish-and-chip shops.

    It is part of the United Kingdom, bordering Wales and Scotland. The Celtic Sea is southwest, while the Irish Sea lies northwest England. The North Sea and the English Channel separate it from continental Europe.

    The country has stunning coastlines and historic sites like York Minster (one of the largest Gothic cathedrals in northern Europe). In addition, the majestic hills of the Lake District and the blissful shores of Cornwall are must-see sites.

    5. Finland

    The skies, the mysticism, the forests, and everything else in Finland have earned it a spot on the list of the most beautiful countries in the world. The country borders Sweden, Norway, and Russia in northern Europe.

    The Northern Lights can be seen from the Arctic Lapland province. Also, the region has a vast wilderness with national parks and ski resorts. Meanwhile, the 18th-century sea fortress Suomenlinna (a unique monument of military architecture) is in its capital city, Helsinki.

    Helsinki is also full of diverse museums, and St. Olaf’s Castle in Savonlinna is a charming historic destination. The castle was built in the 15th century but is still intact and furnished. Also, the three stories Kemi snow castle serves as a hotel and venue for weddings and music concerts.

    6. India

    India is in South Asia and has about 1.38 billion people. It is the second-most populous and seventh-largest country by area. The country's capital city, New Delhi, is the seat of all three branches of the government of India.

    India is known for its wildlife, especially tigers and elephants, lush lagoon networks, and vibrant tea plantations. In addition, the South Asian country has a beautiful terrain that adds to the country's beauty.

    The Indian Ocean beaches and Himalayan peaks make India breathtaking. The Valley of Flowers National Park is exceptional. It contains a variety of species of alpine flowers.

    7. Ireland

    The Irish countryside binds humans with nature. It is an island in the North Atlantic, and the Global Peace Index listed it among the top 15 safest countries in the world in 2020. In addition, the country's mild and moist climate has contributed to the development of the green fields and moss plants in rural Ireland.

    Irish hilltops have the most incredible views in the world. The lakes also enhance the landscape. Also, the Irish castles cannot be overlooked because the ancient designs and unique architecture used on them are a phenomenon.

    Relive old times in Dublin, Cork, and other Irish cities. Ireland is blessed to have sandy beaches and rare rocky coastlines. Moreover, the Irish are the creators of world-class whiskey and beer from Guinness, Jameson, Bushmills, to Kilkenny.

    8. Italy

    Italy is not one of the most visited countries because everything it offers, from stunning ancient architecture to the mountains of Trentino, and the rolling vineyards of Chianti, is magical.

    The country is located in Europe along the Mediterranean coastline. Every visitor going on a trip to Italy never fails to go to the sparkling coast of Amalfi. Italy is also known for having a dozen romantic cities like Rome, Venice, and Florence.

    Its capital, Rome, is a landmark of art and ancient ruins. It is also the home to the Vatican, and Piazza Navona is the city's most charming square. Rome has spaghetti, lasagna, pizza, gelato, and more delicacies. Other than the lovely cities, treat your eyes to the marvelous countryside ruins.

    9. Japan

    Japan is an island country in the northwest Pacific Ocean. The opulent Meiji Shinto Shrine in its capital, Tokyo, is known for its towering gate and surrounding woods. Also, the Imperial Palace sits amid the city's public gardens.

    Tokyo has about 13.96 million people, while the entire country hosts almost 125.8 million human beings. Anyone who has visited Japan will assure you that it is a stunning place. The region has a full range of nature and culture, from ancient temples, snowy mountains, subtropical beaches to futuristic skylines.

    The Noto Peninsula has untouched countryside landscapes and enchanting coastal scenery. Walk on the Kazuabashi Suspension bridge in Shikoku Island. The island also has spectacular coastlines, mountain ranges, and tumbling rivers.

    10. Mexico

    Mexico, officially the United Mexican States, is one of the most beautiful countries to visit, thanks to its long coastlines and remarkable landscape. Its population is around 128.9 million and is known for spicy cuisine, festive drinks, and the best beach destinations in the world.

    The country has more than 60 national parks with wildlife and unusual plants. The deserts and snow-capped volcanoes in the South American country area give a dazzling sight. The state of Oaxaca is one of the most beautiful places in Mexico.

    Beaches are not crowded, and there is a range of dazzling natural rock formations to see. Chiapas has the Sumidero Canyon astounds with 3,000-foot cliff walls, caves, and waterfalls. Get to see butterflies at the Monarch Butterfly Biosphere Reserve.

    11. Morocco

    The North African country is one of the prettiest countries globally and a tourist destination for many, especially those that love adventure. Moreover, it gives you the option of choosing two coastlines, the Atlantic and the Mediterranean.

    Also, not many countries in Africa get to experience the magic of the Sahara like this place. Morocco has desirable locations like Chefchaouen, Imlil Valley, and the High Atlas Mountains.

    The water at the Cap Spartel Lighthouse is blue and rough but perfect for fishing on the reef. You will also witness the rose gold dunes turn to a pinkish and purple color as the sun dips closer to the horizon.

    12. New Zealand

    New Zealand, an island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, is one of the most beautiful countries in the world. The country covers 268,021 square kilometers. It has two main landmasses (the North and South islands) and over 700 smaller islands.

    The Oceania country has an iconic 1500 kilometers of windswept shores, tall mountains, and great turquoise lakes. The trails to the peaks are beautiful too. Wellington city is famous for its award-winning restaurants, tucked-away bars, quirky cafes, and great coffee.

    Not many countries have vast and beautiful fields of pink and purple lupins. Find them at New Zealand's Lake Tekapo or Milford Sound, and explore all of your Lord of the Rings fantasies.

    13. Peru

    Peru is in South America and has around 32.97 million people. A section of the Amazon rainforest is found here. The famous Machu Picchu sits on top of the Andes mountains and deep inside the tropical forest. It is the most spectacular urban creation of the ancient Inca Empire.

    The Rainbow Mountain or Vinicunca is near Cusco. It has unique mineralogy created from a marbling effect. As a result, the mountain has layered hues of gold, red, lavender, and turquoise towering into the sky.

    Peru's capital, Lima (City of Kings), lies on the arid Pacific coast. Here, tradition is the fashion. The Plaza de Armas and the 16th-century cathedral are at the city's heart. Besides these historical sites, enjoy the nightlife of Miraflores, surf at Punta Roquitas, and eat at Central and Maido.

    14. Seychelles

    Seychelles, an African island nation, is on almost every globe-trotter's list for a reason. It is a favorite destination for the A-List Hollywood celebrities and the world's elites, including Prince William and Kate Middleton. The couple spent their honeymoon in Seychelles in 2011.

    Seychelles is an archipelago of 115 islands off East Africa in the Indian Ocean. Here, you will meet rare animals like the giant Aldabra tortoises. Its capital, Victoria, is on the north-eastern side of Mahé island. The British named the town after their Queen Victoria in 1841.

    Thanks to the booming tourism industry, the country is known for its beaches, pristine jungle ecosystems, and super-luxe resorts. Getting Vallée de Mai and Baie Lazare on Mahé feels like touring the Garden of Eden.

    15. Slovenia

    Slovenia deserves the title of the most beautiful country in the world. It is located in Europe, between Italy and Austria, and has a population of 2.1 million. The country's capital, Ljubljana, is located along the Ljubljanica River.

    The city's old town district has many restaurants, bars, cafes, a hill castle, churches, and bridges. Slovenia has magnificent alps, dramatic sceneries, and an exceptional opening to the Mediterranean. Among the extraordinary alps is the Julian Alps.

    Half of Slovenia is covered in forests. The forests have a variety of trees that have an array of green. You will find olive green, apple green, neon green, yellow-green, dark green, khaki green, mint green, and sea green in the trees.

    16. South Africa

    The African landscape is grand, and South Africa offers elegance in its rawest form. Meet the big five animals in South Africa's parks, and Boulders Beach is an excellent place to watch penguins.

    Take an hour's drive from Cape Town, one of South Africa's biggest cities, to the beach. Alternatively, stay in Cape Town and view a colorful skyline filled with Table Mountain and a plateau. The plateau is covered with wildflowers.

    South Africa's population of 59.31 million comprises people speaking diverse local languages such as Afrikaans, Xhosa, Zulu, Southern Sotho, Venda, Tswana, Sotho, Tsonga, Swati, Ndebele. Nevertheless, most South Africans are fluent English speakers.

    17. Spain

    Spain is one of the neatest countries in the world. The natural monuments and ancient buildings outside of the capital enhance the beauty of the Spanish towns. Furthermore, the country has about 15 national parks. Five are in the Canary and Balearic Islands.

    You will find the magnificent Mount Teide (the country's tallest mountain) and Pico Viejo (the world's third tallest volcanic mountain) in Teide National Park.

    18. Sri Lanka

    It is almost impossible to tell that Sri Lanka experienced a civil war because normalcy quickly bounced back. The temple ruins' interior and exterior are appealing. They have historical manuscripts and artifacts that symbolize devotion to a deity.

    Another notable thing in Sri Lanka is the well-maintained tea plantations. The lush green lingers on your eyes as you travel to different parts of the country. Also, Sri Lanka is the pearl of the Indian Ocean. The wide golden beaches are eye-candy, and animal lovers adore the country because its national parks are homes to tons of wildlife.

    19. Switzerland

    Switzerland hosts nature's wonders and is hidden away in the alps. Speaking of mountains, Matterhorn is an appealing sight. Also, the powdery ski slopes and frozen lakes that cover Switzerland, especially during winter, are a charm.

    Villages around the Swiss Alps like Zermatt are incredible, for they have the sweetest air in the world. As a result, the inhabitants of these uncontaminated ski towns sell bottles of air from the mountains.

    Lake Geneva has scenic banks that will make you want to live on the lake. Likewise, Zurich's Badi, natural swimming pools along the lakes and rivers, are a sight to cherish.

    20. The USA

    The US has many natural places you can explore. However, most tourists prefer exploring modern America, especially its biggest cities (Los Angeles, New York, and Chicago), despite the natural and modern sites.

    The Grand Canyon and other parks like Yosemite National Parks are home to many animals. Denali National Park in Alaska is another great place, and Hawaii's vertical Na'Pali Coast is among the world's most fascinating coastlines.
