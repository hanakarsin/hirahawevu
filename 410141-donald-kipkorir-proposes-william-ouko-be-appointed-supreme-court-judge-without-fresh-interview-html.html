    Donald Kipkorir Proposes William Ouko be Appointed Supreme Court Judge Without Fresh Interview

    - Donald Kipkorir disclosed that Justice William Ouko was his best candidate for the chief justice position - Ouko is among the candidates who will be interviewed by JSC for the Supreme Court judge position

    12 Feb 2024, 00:00

    - Donald Kipkorir disclosed that Justice William Ouko was his best candidate for the chief justice position

    - Ouko is among the candidates who will be interviewed by JSC for the Supreme Court judge position

    - He is among judicial officers who have been mounting pressure on President Uhuru Kenyatta to appoint 41 judges

    Flamboyant city lawyer Donald Kipkorir has proposed that Court of Appeal president William Ouko be appointed to the position of Supreme Court judge.

    The vocal lawyer said that Ouko was his best candidate for the chief justice position even as he urged the Judicial Service Commission (JSC) to give him the Supreme Court judge job with immediate effect.

    He said that subjecting Ouko to a fresh interview would amountsto double jeopardy.

    "Justice William Ouko who was my favourite went through the rigorous interview for Chief Justice. For him to prepare again and interview for a seat in Supreme Court is double jeopardy. JSC should use common sense and adopt his interview for CJ mutatis mutandis," Kipkorir tweeted.

    TUKO.co.ke understands that Ouko will be the fifth candidate to appear before JSC on Wednesday, May 5, 2021, for the Supreme Court judge interview.

    On Tuesday, April 27, Kipkorir appeared to endorse Ouko for the chief justice position, which was later in the day handed to Court of Appeal judge, Martha Koome.

    According to Kipkorir, Ouko's leadership would have brought about stability in the judiciary and also regional balance.

    "Looking forward to a new dawn in the Judiciary under the leadership and Presidency of Justice William Ouko as our Chief Justice. His leadership will bring both insider stability and at the same time opening the leadership to other regions," Kipkorir tweeted.

    Kipkorir said that the judiciary requires a leader who will bring and install in it, stability, courtesy, wisdom, sobriety, impartiality, and humility.

    "Most successful Chief Justices are those from within the Judiciary ... Justice William Ouko ticks all boxes," Kipkorir had said on the day that Ouko appeared before JSC for the Chief Justice interview.

    Kipkorir has also lauded the decision by Justice Ogola of Mombasa High Court barring Members of Parliament from representing corruption suspects terming it as an excellent decision.

    "But the ruling should have gone further. The constitution bars State Officers from engaging in any other gainful employment. MPs/Senators are State Officers,” Kipkorir said.

    Other candidates eyeing the Supreme Court judge position include; Chitembwe Juma, Njagi Marete, Mathews Nderi, Patrick Nyaberi, Srgon Kiplagat, and Alice Yano.

    Ouko is among the vocal judicial officers who have been mounting pressure on President Uhuru Kenyatta to appoint 41 judges, 10 of which were nominated to sit at the Court of Appeal.
