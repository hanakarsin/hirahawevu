    Scientists create third embryo of endangered northern white rhino using eggs from Kenya

    - Scientists extracted 10 eggs from remaining female northern white rhinos Najin and Fatu in Ol Pejeta Conservancy - The eggs were flown from Kenya to a laboratory in Italy to be fertilised with preserved frozen sperm

    14 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - Scientists extracted 10 eggs from remaining female northern white rhinos Najin and Fatu in Ol Pejeta Conservancy

    - The eggs were flown from Kenya to a laboratory in Italy to be fertilised with preserved frozen sperm

    - The team created two embryos out of the 10 ova awaiting to be transferred into a surrogate mother

    Hope to save the northern white rhino has been restored after a team of wildlife conservationists successfully created additional pure embryo.

    The scientists drawn from Kenya and Europe had extracted the 10 ova from the remaining female northern white rhinos Najin and Fatu in Ol Pejeta Conservancy, Kenya in August 2019.

    Fatu and Najin are mother and daughter northern white rhinos who live in Ol Pejeta Conservancy and are the remaining two of the species on the planet after death of last male, Sudan, in March 2018.

    After the eggs were harvested, they were flown to a laboratory in Italy to be fertilised with frozen sperm from Suni and Saut, two male northern white rhinos which are son and father.

    Suni died a natural death in 2014 while Saut took his last breath in 2006.

    The procedure involved harvesting immature egg cells (oocytes) from the ovaries of the animals using ultrasound.

    "The procedure has proven to be safe and reproducible and can be performed regularly before the animals become too old. The three embryos are now stored safely and awaiting the next steps of the northern white rhino rescue mission," Ol Pejeta management confirmed.

    The embryo will now be stored in liquid nitrogen awaiting to be transferred into a surrogate mother soon.

    In 2019, scientists announced creation of two similar ova.
